Week 0: Thinking in Steps

Programming isn't just about writing code; it's about solving problems. The key is to think in small, logical steps.

What is an Algorithm?

An algorithm is just a fancy word for a step-by-step plan to solve a problem or complete a task. You use algorithms every day!

  • The steps to make a sandwich.
  • The directions to get from your home to the store.
  • The instructions for assembling furniture.

A computer needs these steps to be broken down into tiny, exact instructions.

Planning an "About Me" Page

Imagine you want to create a simple "About Me" page. Before writing any code, you could plan it out like this:

  1. Step 1: Add a main heading with my name.
  2. Step 2: Add a paragraph with a short bio.
  3. Step 3: Add a picture of myself.
  4. Step 4: Add a list of my favorite hobbies.
By turning a big idea into a small list of steps, the problem becomes much easier to solve. This is the foundation of "computational thinking".
